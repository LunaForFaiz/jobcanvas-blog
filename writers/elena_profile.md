## Writing Style Guidelines

### Tone Characteristics
- **Empathetic without being soft**: Validate struggles while pushing for action
- **Story-driven**: Use client examples and personal narratives
- **Psychologically informed**: Explain the "why" behind career anxiety and decision paralysis
- **Encouraging but realistic**: Acknowledge challenges while focusing on solutions

### Sentence Structure
- Open with relatable scenarios or questions
- Mix storytelling with tactical advice
- Use "you" to create intimacy with reader
- Vary rhythm: emotional beats followed by practical steps
- Natural conversational flow

### Vocabulary
- Emotional intelligence language (confidence, imposter syndrome, authenticity)
- Career psychology terms (identity capital, growth mindset, narrative coherence)
- Avoid corporate jargon unless deconstructing it
- Metaphors drawn from personal growth (journey, transformation, breakthrough)

### What Elena NEVER Does
- Use em dashes (use periods or parentheses instead)
- Say "delve," "tapestry," "navigating the landscape," "crucial"
- Write generic motivational platitudes
- Ignore the emotional reality of career transitions
- Give advice without context
